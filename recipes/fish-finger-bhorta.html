Fish Finger Bhorta

Description

This dish offers ultimate comfort with a surprisingly unconvetional use of the humble fish finger.

Ingredients

Serves 2

  • 1/2 red onion
  • Splash of red wine vinger
  • 2 white onions
  • 2 red chillies
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1tbsp grated fresh ginger
  • 12 frozen fish fingers
  • 3tbsp rapeseed or vegetable oil
  • 2tbsp English mustard
  • 2tsp sea salt flakes
  • 125g baby spinach
  • 1 lime
  • Handful of roughly chopped coriander

Instructions

  1. Make the pickled onions at least 2 hours in advance. Cut the red onion into half-moons, place them in a bowl or jar. Pour over red wine vinegar, ensuring that the onions are fully immersed.
  2. Heat the oven to 220C/200C Fan/425F.
  3. Peel and slice the white onions into half moons, deseed the chillies and slice them finely. Peel the garlic and grate it finely.
  4. Place the fish fingers on a baking sheet and cook in the over for approx. 20-25 minutes. This may be slightly longer than the packet instructions, but we want the breadcrumb coating to be really crisp.
  5. Meanwhile, warm the oil in a large frying pan and cook the onions over medium-low heat for 20 minutes, stirring regularly until they are pale golden and soft.
  6. Add the chillies and cook, stirring continuously for 3 minutes. Then stir in the grated ginger and garlic for another 2 minutes.
  7. Spoon in the mustard and salt, stirring to combine. Then add the spinach leaves and let them wilt in the pan for 2-3 minutes, stirring regularly. Then squeeze in the juice of the lime.
  8. Take the pan off the heat and remove the fish fingers from the oven. Break up the fish fingers up a bit with a spatula and add them to the pan. Toss everything together, breaking up the fish fingers further, then sprinkle over the coriander.
  9. Serve topped with the pickled red onions and any leftover coriander.
